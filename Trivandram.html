Facts About Thrivandram

Thiruvananthapuram (or Thrivandram) is the capital of the state of kerala. its distingushed by its British colonal architecture and many art galleries. Its also home to Kuthira Malika (or puthan malika) Palace,adorned with carved horces and displaying collections related to the Travancore royal family, whose regional capital was here from the 18th-20th centuries. The district was created in 1949, with its headquarters in the city of thiruvananthapuram. The present district was created in 1956 by separaqting the four southernmost taluks of the erstwhile district which are now part of Kanyakumari district.

SREE PADMANABHASWAMY TEMPLE

The padmanabhaswamy temple is a hindu temple locatedin thiruvananthapuram, the state capital of kerala, India. It is considered as the richest place of workship in the world. The name of the city of thiruvananthapuram in malayalam translates to "The city of Lord Ananta",(The city of Lord Vishnu) refering to the deity of the Padmanabhaswamy temple. The templeis builtin an intricate fusion of the Chera style and the Dravidian style of architecture, featuring high walls, and a 16th century gopura. While the Ananthapura temple at kumbla in kazargod is considered as the orginal seat of the Adikesava temple in thiruvananthapuram.

KANANKAKUNNU PALACE

Kananakakunnu palace is one of the most beautiful and historically important places in kerala and is located in the heart of the city, due to which it is easily accessible. It is said to have been built during the rule of Sri Moolam Thirunnalm , who was the then king of the Trivandrum region, in the 19th century.
